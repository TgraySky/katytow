What should I do if my car breaks down on I-10 in Katy? +

If it is safe, ease onto the right shoulder and stop as far from live lanes as possible. Turn on your hazard lights, keep your seatbelt on, and stay inside when traffic is moving fast.

Then open our dispatch form and note a clear landmark—mile marker, exit name (Pin Oak, Peek, Fry), or nearby business. We will call your phone and send the nearest available truck. Do not stand behind the vehicle or walk along I-10 unless you are already in a protected area away from traffic.

Do you provide damage-free towing? +
Yes. For passenger cars, SUVs, and light trucks we prioritize flatbed and wheel-lift methods with proper straps (and soft straps when needed) to help prevent cosmetic and undercarriage damage. Tell us your vehicle type in the callback so we can send the right equipment the first time.

What to Do If You Break Down in Katy, TX

  1. Move to a safe spot: If possible, steer onto the right shoulder or exit the highway completely, away from fast-moving traffic.
  2. Turn on hazard lights: Make sure other drivers can see you—especially at night or during heavy Katy rainstorms.
  3. Stay inside and request help: Remain buckled in, then use our online dispatch form until our team reaches you.
